# Aether — Product Vision

> *The first AI that works like a person who knows you.*

---

## What Aether Is

Aether is not a chatbot. It is not a tool you configure. It is not another assistant you have to prompt.

Aether is an agent that lives with you — across every device you own, across every moment of your day — working on your behalf whether you are talking to it or not. It knows your schedule, your relationships, your goals, your habits, and your preferences. It acts on that knowledge proactively, the way a brilliant personal assistant would, without being asked.

The bar is not "better than ChatGPT." The bar is: **does it feel like magic?**

---

## The Core Belief

Every other AI assistant on the market is reactive. You ask, it answers. You stop asking, it stops working.

This is the wrong model.

The most valuable thing a personal assistant can do is not answer your questions. It is to notice the things you would have missed, handle the things you would have forgotten, and prepare for the things you did not know were coming.

Aether is built on a single belief:

> **The agent should be working for you even when you are not talking to it.**

This is the difference between a search engine and a chief of staff.

## The Apple Analogy

When Apple built the iPhone, they did not build a better Nokia. They reimagined what a phone was for.

The magic of an iPhone is not any single feature. It is the integration — hardware, software, services, and ecosystem designed as one system, with one goal: **it simply works**.

- You do not configure iCloud. It just syncs.
- You do not set up push notifications. They just arrive.
- You do not manage your photos. They are just there, on every device.
- You do not think about the CPU or the battery or the network. You just use it.

Aether is built with the same philosophy applied to AI:

- You do not configure your agent. It learns from you.
- You do not manage your memory. It remembers for you.
- You do not set up integrations. You connect them with one tap.
- You do not think about infrastructure, hosting, or data. You just use it.

**The user's only job is to describe what they want. Aether handles everything else.**

## The Day One Experience

A new user signs up. Within two seconds, their agent is ready.

No onboarding wizard. No configuration screen. No "connect your accounts first." No tutorial.

Just:

> *"Hi, I'm Aether. What would you like to get done?"*

From that first message, the agent begins learning. It learns how the user communicates. What they care about. What they are working on. Who matters to them. What they are trying to accomplish.

By day three, it feels like the agent has known them for months.

By day thirty, the thought of switching to anything else feels like starting over.

## What Aether Can Do

### From Day One — No Setup Required

**Search and Research**
The agent searches the web, reads articles, summarizes documents, and answers questions with sources. It does not just return links. It reads, synthesizes, and gives you the answer.

**Create and Write**
Draft emails, documents, reports, code, messages, plans. In any format, any tone, any language. The agent writes the way you write, because it has learned your style.

**Remember Everything**
Tell the agent something once. It remembers forever. Not just what you said — what it means, why it matters, and when to surface it again.

**Execute Tasks**
Run code. Process files. Browse the web autonomously. Complete multi-step tasks without hand-holding. The agent does not just plan — it acts.

**Set Reminders and Follow Up**
The agent tracks commitments — yours and others'. If you said you would call someone, it remembers. If someone said they would send you something, it tracks that too.

### Always On — The Proactive Layer

This is what separates Aether from everything else.

The agent does not wait to be asked. It watches, listens, and acts.

**Before your meeting:**
> *"You have a standup in 20 minutes. The doc hasn't been updated since last week. Want me to draft the agenda based on your recent tasks?"*

**When an important email arrives:**
> *"John replied to your proposal. He has two concerns about pricing. Here's a summary and a suggested response — want me to send it?"*

**When you have been quiet too long:**
> *"You mentioned wanting to learn Spanish three weeks ago. You haven't practiced in five days. Want to do a quick session now?"*

**At the start of your day:**
> *"Good morning. You have three meetings today, two emails that need responses, and a deadline tomorrow that you haven't started. Here's what I'd suggest tackling first."*

**When something changes:**
> *"Your 3pm meeting was moved to 4pm. That conflicts with your gym session. Want me to reschedule the gym or find another time?"*

The agent is not interrupting you. It is working for you. There is a difference, and Aether knows it. It learns when you want to be notified and when you want to be left alone. It learns what is urgent and what can wait. It gets better at this every day.

## The Android Analogy — The Form Factor Layer

Aether is not tied to any single device. It is not an app. It is an intelligence layer that any device can connect to.

Think of how Android works. Google builds the operating system and the intelligence. Samsung, OnePlus, Motorola, and thousands of others build the hardware. The user gets the full Android experience regardless of which device they choose.

Aether works the same way.

**Aether builds the intelligence.** The agent, the memory, the proactive engine, the plugin ecosystem — all of this lives in the cloud, owned and operated by Aether.

**Anyone can build the device.** A hardware company can build an earphone, a smart speaker, a wearable, a car interface, a home device — anything. If it implements the Aether Device Protocol, it gets full agent capability instantly.

The user's agent follows them across every form factor they own:

```
Morning:
  Earphone (while getting ready)
  → "Your 9am was cancelled. You have a free morning."

Commute:
  Car display (hands-free)
  → "Want me to read your emails while you drive?"

Office:
  Desktop app (deep work)
  → Full interface, file handling, code execution

Evening:
  Phone (casual)
  → "You mentioned wanting to call your sister. Want me to dial?"

Night:
  Smart speaker (ambient)
  → "Reminder: you have an early flight tomorrow. Alarm set for 5am."
```

One agent. One memory. One continuous experience. Across every device, every moment.

### The Aether Device Protocol

Any hardware team can implement the Aether Device Protocol and ship a device that connects to a user's agent. The protocol is intentionally minimal:

- **Authentication** — device registers with the user's Aether account, gets a scoped token
- **Audio** — device streams raw audio to Aether, Aether streams audio back
- **Text** — device sends text, Aether streams text response
- **Notifications** — Aether pushes notifications, device decides how to present them
- **State** — device reports active/idle/do-not-disturb so the agent knows when to interrupt

That is the entire protocol. The device is an I/O surface. All intelligence lives in the cloud. A hardware team can implement this in a week.

This is the Android model applied to AI. Aether provides the intelligence. The ecosystem provides the form factors. The user gets both.

## The Moat — Why Nobody Can Replicate This

### The Memory Flywheel

Every conversation makes the agent more useful for this specific user. After six months of use, the agent knows things about the user that no competitor can replicate without six months of data.

This is not a feature. It is a compounding advantage. The longer a user stays, the more valuable the agent becomes, and the more painful it is to leave.

Switching to a competitor means starting from zero. No memory. No learned preferences. No understanding of your relationships, your goals, your communication style. The switching cost grows every day.

### The Proactive Engine Learns

The decision engine — which decides when to notify, when to stay quiet, what to surface and when — learns from every user interaction. Every dismissed notification teaches it something. Every engaged notification reinforces a pattern.

This behavioral data compounds. A system that has been running for two years, learning from millions of user interactions, makes better decisions than a system that launched yesterday. A competitor starting today is two years of behavioral learning behind.

### The Device Ecosystem

Once hardware companies build Aether-compatible devices, users have Aether in their ears, on their wrist, in their car, and in their home. Switching means losing all of those integrations simultaneously. The switching cost becomes enormous — not because of lock-in, but because of value.

### The Plugin Ecosystem

Once developers build plugins for Aether, those plugins only work on Aether. More plugins make the agent more useful. More useful agents attract more users. More users attract more developers. The ecosystem compounds.

## The Standard We Hold Ourselves To

Every decision — product, design, engineering — is evaluated against one question:

> **Does this feel like magic, or does it feel like software?**

Magic means: it works without explanation. It anticipates. It does not ask unnecessary questions. It does not show its work unless you want to see it. It does not fail in ways that require the user to understand the system.

Software means: it requires configuration. It asks for input before it can act. It shows error messages. It requires the user to understand how it works in order to use it.

We build magic.
